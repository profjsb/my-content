Sam Charrington interviews Joshua Bloom, UC Berkeley astronomy professor and co-founder/CTO of Wise.io, about his path from time-domain astrophysics to building a production machine learning company. Bloom describes pioneering ML for real-time classification of variable stars and transients from robotic telescope surveys (including fully automated discovery-to-follow-up pipelines that led to finding the nearest Type Ia supernova in 25 years), and how those lessons shaped Wise.io's pivot from data science tooling to ML-powered customer support (ticket triage, suggested and automatic responses). He details Wise.io's stack — C++ core algorithms surfaced in Python, per-customer models, Docker-containerized data science pipelines with contract-based "organizational APIs" — and their open source paratext parallel CSV reader. The conversation closes with his ideas on cost-optimized AI (accuracy as mere table stakes versus RAM, compute, and labor costs), model interpretability and "reason codes," and balancing startup CTO life with academia.
