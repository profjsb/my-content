TWIML Talk #42 (2017): "Marrying Physics-Based and Data-Driven ML Models with Josh Bloom." Sam Charrington catches up with Josh Bloom, the UC Berkeley astronomer turned Wise.io founder, now VP of Data and Analytics at GE Digital after GE's late-2016 acquisition of Wise.io. Bloom discusses industrial machine learning at GE scale — jet engine predictive maintenance with extremely rare failure events, reframing rare-anomaly problems as assistive ranked-triage tools, autoencoders for unsupervised feature generation from time-series sensor data, and GANs as a privacy-preserving way to share synthetic stand-ins for sensitive data. The core theme is combining physics-based models with data-driven ML — feeding physical-model predictions (or residuals after subtracting the known physics) into ML models — illustrated with wind turbine optimization, digital twins, and a continual/reinforcement learning spectrum, arguing the hybrid approach beats purely data-driven models when failure data is scarce.
