In this May 2024 A3D3 seminar, Bloom argues that despite ML being embedded in astronomy's "midstream" (post-data-collection inference), the real AI revolution has not yet arrived. He reviews mature successes — real/bogus transient detection (now a solved deep-learning problem), self-supervised autoencoders and periodicity-aware "polar coordinate" convolutions for variable-star classification with astronomy's "small label problem", and likelihood-free/simulation-based inference with autoregressive flows that produced exoplanet microlensing posteriors 100,000x faster than MCMC and led to the discovery of a previously unknown mathematical degeneracy in the gravitational lens equation. He then maps the white spaces: upstream AI (cosmic-ray removal with autoencoders, RL for adaptive optics and LSST/Rubin control loops, sensor fusion for sky transparency prediction, RL-based follow-up of gravitational-wave localizations) and downstream human augmentation, including LLM-generated source summaries and embedding-based similarity search in SkyPortal, with multimodal foundation models for time-domain data as the open frontier.
