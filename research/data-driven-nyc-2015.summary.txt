Speaking at FirstMark's Data Driven NYC (November 2015) as co-founder/CTO of Wise.io, Josh Bloom asks where the "build vs. buy" boundary should sit for AI in organizations, drawing on his path from astronomy's century-old big-data problem (Harvard's human "computers") to building an ML layer on top of SaaS customer-support tools like Zendesk and Salesforce. He argues that production machine learning is a multi-axis optimization — accuracy vs. interpretability, implementability, memory/speed, time-to-deploy, and maintenance cost — citing the Netflix Prize winner that was never deployed and Google's "high-interest credit card of technical debt" paper (95% of production ML code is glue code). He advocates "fault-tolerant machine learning" with feedback loops that move from augmentation (suggesting top ticket responses, instant triage/routing) toward automation as confidence grows, noting Wise.io serves millions of predictions monthly and that even Google and Netflix ship imperfect ML. A Q&A covers enterprise selling (ROI first, data scientists as influencers) and why bounded natural-language support problems are harder than they look, with featurization mattering more than model choice.
